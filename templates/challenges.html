Challenges Facing Bees and Beekeeping

Despite their resilience, bees face numerous threats in the modern world. Pesticides, particularly neonicotinoids, have been linked to colony collapse disorder (CCD), where entire hives die off suddenly. Habitat destruction, driven by urbanization and intensive agriculture, has diminished the availability of wildflowers and nesting sites. Climate change, with its erratic weather patterns, disrupts flowering cycles and forage availability.

Beekeepers must contend with additional challenges, such as the spread of the Varroa destructor mite, which weakens bees and transmits deadly viruses. Yet, they remain committed to protecting their colonies, employing methods such as integrated pest management, supplemental feeding, and selective breeding for disease-resistant bees.
